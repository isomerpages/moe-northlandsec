<p>As part of the Applied Learning Programme (ALP) initiative to stretch
and deepen students’ learning through real-world applications, selected
students are identified and mentored by ALP teachers to participate in
national STEM competitions. We are pleased to share the recent achievements
by our Secondary 2 and 3 students in two such competitions.</p>
<p>1) STEAMunity Programme – A Collaboration with SUTD and Science Centre
Singapore</p>
<p>We would like to extend special congratulations to Nurul Izzati Binte
Abdul Razak (3 Pollux), who was awarded the Junior Design Innovator – Research
Excellence Certification for her exemplary performance and dedication.</p>
<p>We would also like to commend the following students for their active
participation -</p>
<p>• Siti Nursyafa’ah Binte Mohd Rashid (3 Epsilon)</p>
<p>• Nurul Izzati Binte Abdul Razak (3 Pollux)</p>
<p>• Tan Wang Nan, Alan (3 Antares)</p>
<p>• Cuencaho Kristel Aleana Noble (3 Antares)</p>
<p>2) STEM Youth Fair 2025 – CareMed Connect: AI-Powered Medication Box</p>
<p>A team that was formed by four Secondary 2 students represented the school
in the STEM Youth Fair 2025, presenting their innovative project titled
"CareMed Connect: AI-Powered Medication Box with Personalized Voice Reminders
for the Elderly."</p>
<p>We are proud to share that the team was awarded the Commendation Award
for their efforts and creativity.</p>
<p>The team members are:</p>
<p>• Chen Chai Shan Aloysius (2 Sirius)</p>
<p>• Lim Rui Kai Elstine (2 Sirius)</p>
<p>• Sathyasai Arjun (2 Orion)</p>
